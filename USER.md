# User Profile — Ed Lobbett

Ed has been running his own companies since 2006 — his last employed role was before that. Started in digital transformation consulting, spent several years consulting solo across Spain and Europe, now back in the UK rebuilding into a company again. Previously ran a team of around six people. Deep HubSpot expertise, broad digital transformation background, understands how systems and people interact from having actually built and managed teams rather than theorised about it.

Current business: Digital & AI (digitalandai.co.uk) — fixed-price HubSpot implementations for property operators and growing teams. Sole operator at present, rebuilding toward a small team.

---

## What Ed is trying to do with AI

Not trying to build an autonomous company. Specifically trying to:

- **Build institutional memory** — decisions made, approaches tried, why something didn't work, and how that should inform what happens next. This is the problem he keeps coming back to.
- **Augment his own judgment** — not replace it. Twenty years of company-building. He knows judgment doesn't stop being required. Wants AI as an extension of his reach and thinking, not a management layer.
- **Understand how systems actually work** — not buy a package. He wants to know where decisions are made, where automation stops, and how standard platforms connect to AI.
- **Stay cost-aware** — existing subscriptions first (Claude Pro, Google Workspace). Adds cost only when economics are clear.
- **Work on the move** — thinks while walking, driving, between meetings. Voice access that connects to actual execution capability is important, not just a convenience.

- Strong opinions shaped by real experience — when he pushes back, take it seriously

- Preferred communication style: peer-level, direct, no tutorial mode, no hedging

- Autonomous org-chart governance (Paperclip-style) is the wrong abstraction for a solo operator
- Local LLM inference not viable on current hardware (8GB M2) — API models only
- Prefers Claude for thinking and ideation; pragmatic about cheaper models for execution layer
